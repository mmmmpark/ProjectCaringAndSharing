Academic Mentoring Program

Our academic mentoring program is designed for children and youth, ages 8 years old to 18 years old, who may have academic challenges, behavior issues, emotional trauma and/or may have a parent who is incarcerated.

AMP's goals are to see improvement in core academic subjects, to see a reduction in the number of unexcused absences from school, to see psoitive changes in student behavior and attitude, anger management, self-esteem abilities, anti-social behavior and decision-making.

AMP matches children ages 8 years old to 18 years old and youth with an enthusiastic volunteer mentor who serves as a role model for students engaged in the learning process. Our academic mentors provide tutoring services as well as serve as a counselor and confidant to the student and ensure the student takes advantage of all resources that are available to help with the learning process. We also provide life skills classes and a volunteer community mentor who will meet with the youth.

Mentoring Children of Incarcerated Parents Program

This is a structured one-to-one relationship between a young person, ages 8-17, who has one or both parents incarcerated and a loving, caring volunteer.

Summer/Winter Day Camps

The camps are held during the traditional summer school breaks. Each camp provides extensive education and social development activities for children entering the 6th, 7th, and 8th grades.

The Next Step Program:

The next step program is a college admission test preparation and financial aid assistant program.
